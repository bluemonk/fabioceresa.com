Hello, My Name is Fabio.

Fabio Ceresa, born 1981. Young lombard stage director and librettist grown up at La Scala of Milan. Highly motivated Opera artisan.

At Teatro alla Scala in Milan he works side by side with some of the most important modern directors - Luca Ronconi, Deborah Warner, Patrice Chéreau, Dmitri Tcherniakov, Pierluigi Pizzi, Peter Stein, Eimuntas Nekrosius, Richard Jones – also participating in the restaging of historical productions by Giorgio Strehler, Franco Zeffirelli and Jean Pierre Ponnelle, both in in national and international tours.

He makes his debut as director signing in 2010 Madama Butterfly for the Fondazione Pergolesi Spontini in Jesi. In 2012 he stages for Opera Lombardia a new production of La traviata and signs the dramaturgy and direction of Luigi Rossi’s Orfeo for the Valle d’Itria Festival, where the year after he is reinvited for staging Verdi’s Giovanna d’Arco. In 2014 he directs Tosca at Teatro Coccia in Novara, and Madama Butterfly at Teatro del Maggio Musicale Fiorentino in Florence. In 2015 He signs a new coproduction of I Puritani for Florence and Teatro Regio in Tourin, as well as a new Madama Butterfly for Florence and Teatro Petruzzelli in Bari. The 2015 season of Wexford Festival Opera invites him to stage Mascagni’s Guglielmo Ratcliff.

Resume (english)

Fabio Ceresa, born 1981. Young lombard stage director and librettist grown up at La Scala of Milan. Highly motivated Opera artisan.

At Teatro alla Scala in Milan he works side by side with some of the most important modern directors - Luca Ronconi, Deborah Warner, Patrice Chéreau, Dmitri Tcherniakov, Pierluigi Pizzi, Peter Stein, Eimuntas Nekrosius, Richard Jones – also participating in the restaging of historical productions by Giorgio Strehler, Franco Zeffirelli and Jean Pierre Ponnelle, both in in national and international tours.

He makes his debut as director signing in 2010 Madama Butterfly for the Fondazione Pergolesi Spontini in Jesi. In 2012 he stages for Opera Lombardia a new production of La traviata and signs the dramaturgy and direction of Luigi Rossi’s Orfeo for the Valle d’Itria Festival, where the year after he is reinvited for staging Verdi’s Giovanna d’Arco. In 2014 he directs Tosca at Teatro Coccia in Novara, and Madama Butterfly at Teatro del Maggio Musicale Fiorentino in Florence. In 2015 He signs a new coproduction of I Puritani for Florence and Teatro Regio in Tourin, as well as a new Madama Butterfly for Florence and Teatro Petruzzelli in Bari. The 2015 season of Wexford Festival Opera invites him to stage Mascagni’s Guglielmo Ratcliff.

At the same time, he works intensely as librettist. Together with Marco Tutino, he writes La Ciociara, commissioned by San Francisco Opera, published for Sonzogno Editions and performing in 2015. He has written the libretto for Daniele Zanettovich’s Marco Polo, published by Ed. Musicali Pizzicato and performed in 2014 at Teatro Nazionale in Fiume. Librettos for Daniela Terranova are entirely published by Suvini Zerboni. In 2010, the singspiel Re Tuono, set to music by Daniela Terranova, wins the first prize of the competition Kinderszenen held by the Comitato Nazionale Italiano Musica (CIDIM); in 2011, the opera Mannaggia a Bubbà! wins the first prize of the “Gianni Bergamo Music Award”. After the great success of Luigi Rossi’s Orfeo, the Paolo Grassi Foundation has commissioned a new opera entitled Le falene for the 2013 season.

He has collaborated with Marco Taralli, writing many works for Teatro Carlo Felice in Genova, Teatro Comunale in Bologna Festival Monteverdi in Cremona, Gran Teatre del Liceu in Barcelona, Auditorium Parco della Musica and Teatro dell’Opera in Rome.
