Hello, My Name is Fabio.

Fabio Ceresa, born 1981. Young lombard stage director and librettist. Highly motivated Opera artisan, winner of the 2016 International Opera Award for Young Director.

At Teatro alla Scala in Milan he works side by side with some of the most important modern directors - Luca Ronconi, Deborah Warner, Patrice Chéreau, Dmitri Tcherniakov, Pierluigi Pizzi, Peter Stein, Eimuntas Nekrosius, Richard Jones – also participating in the restaging of historical productions by Giorgio Strehler, Franco Zeffirelli and Jean Pierre Ponnelle, both in national and international tours.

Winner of the 2016 International Opera Award for Young Director, he makes his debut as director signing in 2010 Madama Butterfly for the Fondazione Pergolesi Spontini in Jesi. In 2015 He signs a new coproduction of I Puritani for Florence and Teatro Regio in Tourin, as well as a new Madama Butterfly for Teatro del Maggio Musicale Fiorentino in Florence and Teatro Petruzzelli in Bari. The 2015 season of Wexford Festival Opera invites him to stage Mascagni’s Guglielmo Ratcliff, which has also been nominiated in the Rediscovered Work category of the 2016 International Opera Award. In 2016 he signs Orlando Finto Pazzo for Korean National Opera.

Resume

At Teatro alla Scala in Milan he works side by side with some of the most important modern directors - Luca Ronconi, Deborah Warner, Patrice Chéreau, Dmitri Tcherniakov, Pierluigi Pizzi, Peter Stein, Eimuntas Nekrosius, Richard Jones – also participating in the restaging of historical productions by Giorgio Strehler, Franco Zeffirelli and Jean Pierre Ponnelle, both in national and international tours.

Winner of the 2016 International Opera Award for Young Director, he makes his debut as director signing in 2010 Madama Butterfly for the Fondazione Pergolesi Spontini in Jesi. In 2015 He signs a new coproduction of I Puritani for Florence and Teatro Regio in Tourin, as well as a new Madama Butterfly for Teatro del Maggio Musicale Fiorentino in Florence and Teatro Petruzzelli in Bari. The 2015 season of Wexford Festival Opera invites him to stage Mascagni’s Guglielmo Ratcliff, which has also been nominiated in the Rediscovered Work category of the 2016 International Opera Award. In 2016 he signs Orlando Finto Pazzo for Korean National Opera.

His future engagements include Rigoletto Theater Kiel and a return to Wexford Festival Opera to direct Donizetti’s Maria di Rudenz.

At the same time, he works intensely as librettist. Together with Marco Tutino, he writes La Ciociara, commissioned by San Francisco Opera, published for Sonzogno Editions and performing in 2015. He has written the libretto for Daniele Zanettovich’s Marco Polo, published by Ed. Musicali Pizzicato and performed in 2014 at Teatro Nazionale in Fiume. Librettos for Daniela Terranova are entirely published by Suvini Zerboni. In 2010, the singspiel Re Tuono, set to music by Daniela Terranova, wins the first prize of the competition Kinderszenen held by the Comitato Nazionale Italiano Musica (CIDIM); in 2011, the opera Mannaggia a Bubbà! wins the first prize of the “Gianni Bergamo Music Award”. After the great success of Luigi Rossi’s Orfeo, the Paolo Grassi Foundation has commissioned a new opera entitled Le falene for the 2013 season.

He has collaborated with Marco Taralli, writing many works for Teatro Carlo Felice in Genova, Teatro Comunale in Bologna Festival Monteverdi in Cremona, Gran Teatre del Liceu in Barcelona, Auditorium Parco della Musica and Teatro dell’Opera in Rome.
